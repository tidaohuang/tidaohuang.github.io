The Suggested Number Of Direct/Indirect Labor with Empirical Data on the Second Half of 2018

Linear Model

Abstract

The analysis applies the data related to direct/indirect labors on the second half of 2018 to formulate the proper linear model so that we can predict the appropriate number of direct/indirect labor in 2019, and this prediction may support managers in the shop floor environment to make a decision of the worker assignment. The rest of the present work is organized as follows. In section I, we pinpoint that the production manager may determine the need of direct/indirect labor by rule of thumb, and this decision may not satisfy the feasibility and validity or maintain the efficiency of the real manufacturing system. In section II, the descriptive statistics is used for discover the difference between the number of labor in day shift and that in night shift. Linear regression is utilized as one of the methodology to predict the appropriate number of labors in section III, and the key evaluation criterion we consider is R-square. The result and the discussion are given in section IV, and the conclusion is shown in section V.

Completion time: May, 2019

IPO model

01. Input

  • The information of punching in of labors (CSV file)
  • Production quantity data (CSV file)
  • Machine usage history data (CSV file)

02. Process

  • Perform descriptive statistics of the data
  • Fit linear regression model to predict the need of labor
  • Detect potential outlier, unusual observation, and collinearity problem
  • Compare the performance of different regression model by R-square

03. Output

  • Final fitted linear model of direct/indirect labor in different case

Benefit and Result

  • Discover the difference pattern between day shift and night shift
  • The fitted model can be used to predict the appropriate number of direct/indirect labor in 2019.
